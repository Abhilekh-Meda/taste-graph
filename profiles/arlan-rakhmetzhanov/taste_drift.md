# Evolution of Arlan Rakhmetzhanov's Taste and Judgment

Based on public statements from 2023-2026, Arlan Rakhmetzhanov's journey shows several clear shifts in thinking—particularly around execution strategy, team building, and the role of planning. What's notable is his **unusual willingness to publicly acknowledge mistakes** for someone so young.

## Early Positions Later Reversed or Softened

### **"Build fast, skip planning" → "Planning is more important than code"**

**December 2024 position**: In his year-end reflection, Rakhmetzhanov harshly criticized his first startup DresserAI for over-planning:

> "For over three months, my team and I focused on customer development and refining our pitch deck, but all our effort led nowhere... We didn't know our next move and somehow hoped someone would just hand us $50,000. Lesson learned: this approach will never work." [Source 8]

He advocated for pure execution: "move fast and break things" and "avoid getting lost in endless ideas and possibilities that never materialize into real, tangible products." [Source 8]

**November 2024 position** (one month earlier, interestingly): He released "Nia Oracle" with this statement:

> "Planning is more important than writing actual code... Mistakes in the research phase become a flood of silent bugs. Every missing file and every hidden dependency turns into a future headache. If you're frustrated with the agent's output, go back and review the plan, not the implementation." [Source 6]

**Analysis**: This isn't a contradiction but a **maturation in thinking**. He evolved from "planning without building is wasteful" (DresserAI lesson) to "planning *before* building prevents bigger problems" (Nozomio lesson). The shift shows he learned to distinguish between *overthinking ideas* versus *properly architecting solutions*.

---

### **Solo founder pride → "I would not recommend this path"**

**March 2025 position**: Rakhmetzhanov posted about running his startup "completely solo for almost a year," listing benefits like enhanced multitasking and faster iteration. But he added a crucial caveat:

> "It is like playing Minecraft on ultra-hardcore mode with 3 hearts instead of 10. It will stress you out, put you through agony, and force discipline on you like nothing else... I would not recommend this path to most people." [Source 5]

**Recent position (2026)**: Now actively hiring:
> "P.S. I am hiring founding team members: growth interns, researchers, and cracked engineers. DM me!" [Source 5]

And from his Business Insider interview: "I plan to use the funds to make my first hire once I can't handle all of Nozomio's customers singlehandedly." [Source 12]

**Evolution**: He shifted from **necessity-driven solo work** to recognizing it as unsustainable, though he still values staying lean ("the leanest AI startup in terms of talent density").

---

### **Domain exploration without expertise → "Look where you have expertise"**

**Admitted mistake** (December 2024):
> "I've been there myself, trying to go into fields like quantum physics or blockchain—areas where I was completely dumb. To launch a successful startup, you need at least some foundational knowledge in the domain you're tackling." [Source 8]

He cited Paul Graham's essay "How to Get Up Startup Ideas" as influential, specifically the quote: "When searching for ideas, look in areas where you have some expertise." [Source 8]

**Current approach**: Focused on coding agents and context engineering—areas where he has direct experience as a developer who codes "5k+ lines of code per day." [Source 1]

## Clear Shifts in Values Over Time

### **Location: Kazakhstan/London → San Francisco commitment**

**October 2024 realization**:
> "Honestly, not being in SF has been tough. I've realized that being there is critical if I want to maximize my chances of building a unicorn as fast as possible. I am glad I am coming back because one day in SF is worth three to five days anywhere else (and I noticed the same with revenue)." [Source 3]

**Current position**: After getting his O-1 visa, he's now based in SF and calls it "the best city on earth." [Source 3]

**What changed**: He went from building remotely in Kazakhstan (2024) to treating **physical proximity to the SF ecosystem as non-negotiable** for success.

---

### **Education: Stanford dreams → Dropout with no regrets**

**2024 goal**: "My main goal has always been to get into Stanford. I was laser-focused—I cold-emailed over 300 professors, and eventually became a research assistant to Professor Ilya Strebulaev." [Source 5]

**March 2025**: Dropped out of 11th grade to attend Y Combinator [Source 2]

**January 2026 reflection**:
> "Both were dream schools of mine about 10 months ago, when I was stressed about my SAT and final exams. I never imagined that I would be speaking there, not as a student, but as a guest." [Source 11]

When his mother was "pissed for a solid week" about dropping out, he told her he might return to school. But after arriving in SF: "that's when I told her I was dropping out for good." [Source 12]

**No regrets expressed**—in fact, he frames it as an advantage: "Being a young founder has definite advantages... being young gives you a head start. There's still time if you fail." [Source 12]

## Public Acknowledgments of Being Wrong

Unusually for a teenage founder, Rakhmetzhanov explicitly catalogs his mistakes:

1. **DresserAI's planning paralysis**: "Lesson learned: this approach will never work" [Source 8]

2. **Skipping coding fundamentals**: "When I first got hooked on coding, I skipped some basics—which I now regret, hence why I'm taking Harvard's CS50 now" [Source 8]

3. **Health neglect**: "That turned out badly. I won't go into details, but let's just say I'm dealing with issues related to iron and other nutrient deficiency." [Source 8]

4. **Unrealistic domain choices**: Admitted trying "areas where I was completely dumb" [Source 8]

His December 2024 essay is framed as "What I Learned In 2024" with the subtitle "Reflection on my ups and downs"—**explicitly positioning himself as learning publicly** rather than projecting infallibility. [Source 8]

## Implications

Rakhmetzhanov's evolution suggests a **fast-feedback learning loop** rather than rigid ideology:

1. He tests approaches (solo building, location independence, planning vs. execution)
2. Notices what works via concrete metrics (revenue differences in SF, user feedback)
3. **Publicly updates his beliefs** within months

His willingness to write "I was completely dumb" [Source 8] about past choices—and to warn others against paths he took—indicates **judgment maturing faster than typical** for his age, likely accelerated by the high-stakes startup environment.

The shift from "avoid planning" to "planning is more important than code" within a single month (Nov-Dec 2024) shows he's **synthesizing lessons in real-time** rather than following a predetermined playbook.
