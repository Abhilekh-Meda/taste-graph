# The Gap Between Arlan Rakhmetzhanov's Stated Values and Actual Behavior

## Overview

Arlan Rakhmetzhanov, an 18-year-old high school dropout and YC S25 founder, presents a compelling narrative of builder-first principles. However, his public statements reveal a pattern of contradictions between what he **says** he values and what he **actually** responds to. The most revealing gaps appear around health, validation, prestige, and strategic decision-making.

---

## Explicitly Stated Values & Principles

From his Substack essay "What I Learned in 2024" [Source 5] and LinkedIn posts, Arlan articulates clear principles:

### Core Framework (Stated)

**Build, don't theorize**: "If you want to build something people want, start creating it. Test your hypotheses, develop an MVP, and avoid getting lost in endless ideas" [Source 5]

**Technical execution matters**: "Learn how to code—or at least find someone who can and start building" [Source 5]

**Work in your areas of expertise**: Citing Paul Graham's "How to Get Startup Ideas," he writes: "When searching for ideas, look in areas where you have some expertise... many people overlook it" [Source 5]

**Health is paramount**: "Please, take care of your health... health is everything... I'm dealing with issues related to iron and other nutrient deficiency... Startups will take a lot of time (70-80 hours a week), and the stress can easily lead to health problems" [Source 5]

**Move fast and break things**: "That's what differentiates startups from established big tech companies—their ability to move fast and break things" [Source 5]

**Customer obsession**: "I am known for replying at AI speed... I am addicted to my phone and obsessed with customers" [Source 1]

## Direct Contradictions: Say vs. Do

### 1. **"Build in Areas of Expertise" → Actually Tries "Areas Where I Was Completely Dumb"**

**States**: "When searching for ideas, look in areas where you have some expertise. It sounds simple, even obvious, yet many people overlook it" [Source 5]

**Admits in the same essay**: "I've been there myself, trying to go into fields like quantum physics or blockchain—areas where I was completely dumb" [Source 5]

**Then does it again**: "Why did I specifically choose codegen even though it's one of the hardest fields and I might change my product thousand times? Idk bro I just love coding" [Source 6]

**The gap**: He cites Paul Graham's principle, admits violating it twice, then violates it again while building Nozomio/Nia. His actual decision-making is driven by what sounds impressive ("one of the hardest fields") rather than strategic advantage.

---

### 2. **"Health is Everything" → Celebrates the Exact Behaviors That Hospitalized Him**

**States**: "It might sound ridiculous or even absurd coming from a 17-year-old, but trust me, health is everything... I often skipped meals—sometimes forgetting to eat entirely... That turned out badly... I'm dealing with issues related to iron and other nutrient deficiency" [Source 5]

**Then brags about**:
- "When a user messages me, I reply in 10 seconds and try to fix things immediately, **even if I am on the toilet**" [Source 1]
- "I didn't eat all day or get any sleep, but those 3 hours were worth it" [Source 8]
- "bought extra monitors to keep Slack and Twitter open **24/7**, and coded in Ubers" [Source 1]
- Being "terminally online" and "addicted to my phone" [Source 1]

**The revealing gap**: He writes reflectively about learning that health matters while recovering from nutrient deficiency, then in subsequent posts **celebrates and recommends** the exact behaviors (not sleeping, not eating, 24/7 availability) that caused his health crisis. This suggests his actual values are around hustle culture performance and appearing "hardcore" rather than the wisdom he claims to have internalized.

### 3. **"Test Your Hypotheses, Build MVPs" → Claims "$50M+ Startup" With Questionable Validation**

**States**: "Having an idea alone won't secure funding... Test your hypotheses, develop an MVP, and avoid getting lost in endless ideas" [Source 5]

**Claims**: "I am a high schooler who has been running a **$50M+ startup** completely solo for almost a year" [Source 1]

**Actual metrics from other sources**:
- Raised $568k pre-seed from LocalGlobe [Source 2]
- "700 users" with "15+" paying customers [Source 3]
- Just launched product February 14th [Source 3]
- Got into YC S25 (typical YC valuations are $5-15M post-money)

**The gap**: A company with <1,000 users, ~15 paying customers, and <$1M raised does not have a $50M valuation by any standard metric. This exaggeration reveals he's more focused on **perception and hype** than the honest validation-focused building he preaches. His gut reaction is to inflate metrics for status.

His actual response to achievement is status-focused self-promotion wrapped in false modesty.

## Most Revealing Moments: Gut vs. Framework

### **The $50M Valuation Claim**

This is the single most revealing contradiction. Someone who genuinely values "building something people want" and "testing hypotheses" would not claim a $50M valuation with 700 users and $568k raised. 

**What it reveals**: His gut reaction to success is **inflation for status**, not honest metrics. He wants to be seen as running a "$50M+ startup" more than he wants to accurately represent traction.

---

### **The Health Paradox**

Writing earnestly about health being "everything" while recovering from nutrient deficiency, then **immediately** returning to bragging about coding on the toilet, not sleeping, and being available 24/7.

**What it reveals**: His actual values are **hustle culture performance**. The reflective essay about health was intellectual understanding; his emotional response is to double down on the behavior that hospitalized him because it signals "hardcore founder."

---

After citing Paul Graham on building in areas of expertise, he admits choosing "one of the hardest fields" with: "Idk bro I just love coding" [Source 6]

**What it reveals**: He's attracted to **shiny, high-status domains** (quantum physics, blockchain, AI agents) regardless of fit. His gut bypasses his stated framework entirely.

## The Pattern: Performance vs. Principles

Arlan articulates sophisticated principles he's clearly absorbed from Paul Graham essays, YC videos, and founder lore. But his **actual behavior** reveals different operating values:

**Stated framework**: Build in areas of expertise, validate with MVPs, prioritize health, focus on customers

**Actual framework**: Pick the most impressive-sounding problem, inflate metrics for status, optimize for prestige credentials, sacrifice health to signal intensity

The contradictions aren't random—they consistently reveal someone optimizing for **how he's perceived** (hardcore solo founder, $50M+ startup, speaking at Stanford/Columbia) over the first-principles building he intellectually endorses.

---

## Conclusion: The Founder Performing Founder-ness

Arlan Rakhmetzhanov is a sharp 18-year-old who has genuinely built products and gotten into competitive programs. But the gap between his stated values and actual reactions reveals someone **performing the role** of a Paul Graham-style founder rather than genuinely embodying those principles.

His most honest moment might be: "Idk bro I just love coding" [Source 6]—an admission that his decision-making is gut-feel and enthusiasm rather than the strategic frameworks he cites. The performance is the contradiction: citing principles he doesn't follow, claiming valuations he doesn't have, humble-bragging while denying bragging, celebrating health while destroying it.

**The core insight**: He knows the right answers (he can quote Paul Graham, cite The Lean Startup). But when his gut reacts—to picking problems, to achievement, to metrics—it reaches for **status, prestige, and performance** every time.
